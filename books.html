A Measure of Breath

On a train journey to a church disciplinary meeting, a minister carries the weight of illness, memory, and the quiet ache of betrayal.

A Measure of Breath is a contemplative novel exploring faith, survival, justice, leadership, and the stories our bodies carry long after words have failed.

“A Measure of Breath is a powerful, personal reflection on survival—the damage we carry, and the light that still burns.” — Stephen Tomkins, Editor, Reform Magazine

Why This Book

This novel emerged from questions of survival, memory, faith, illness, and hope. It asks what remains when certainty falls away, and whether grace can still be found in the ordinary work of breathing, remembering, and continuing.

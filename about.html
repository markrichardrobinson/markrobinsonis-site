About

Mark Richard Robinson

Thank you for taking the time to visit my website.

I am a minister, writer, artist, and public theologian. Much of my work begins with ordinary life: the memories we hold, the hope we inhabit, and the struggles we face.

My work is rooted in scripture, justice, liberation, healing, and the dignity of human community. I am drawn to theology that speaks truthfully about life in its struggle and joy, while still daring to hope in the future God is bringing into being.

Through sermons, essays, visual art, and published work, I explore questions of faith, memory, embodiment, suffering, hope, and the presence of God within human experience.

I serve congregations within the United Reformed Church in the United Kingdom and am the author of A Measure of Breath , a contemplative novel shaped by survival, memory, faith, and hope.
